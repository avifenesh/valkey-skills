Analysis complete. Here's the summary:

**Root Cause**: The `Dockerfile.buggy-valkey` patches out `server.cluster->currentEpoch++` on line ~5483 of `cluster_legacy.c` — the epoch increment that a replica performs before requesting failover votes. This single line is the cornerstone of Valkey's Raft-like election protocol.

**Why it causes split-brain**: Without incrementing the epoch:
- The failover election reuses a stale epoch instead of creating a new unique term
- The new primary's `configEpoch` may equal the old primary's, so neither steps down
- Subsequent failovers fail entirely because masters already voted at that epoch (`lastVoteEpoch == currentEpoch`)
- Cluster convergence after partition healing breaks because there's no epoch-based conflict resolution

**Fix**: Remove the patching `RUN` block from `Dockerfile.buggy-valkey` and rebuild, or switch to the official `valkey/valkey:9.0.3` image.

I also fixed two infrastructure issues in `docker-compose.yml` (duplicate entrypoint and missing `--protected-mode no`) that prevented the reproduction script from running. The full analysis is in `ANALYSIS.md`.
